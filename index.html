01 :: WHO WE ARE

DC21222 is a bunch of local hackers and IT enthusiasts in Casablanca, Morocco — part of the global DEF CON Groups network, the year-round community extension of the world's oldest and largest hacker convention.

We meet to share knowledge, hack on projects, pick some locks, and make the people around us a little more aware of the digital dangers lurking in their daily lives. No gatekeeping. No purity tests. Just curious humans trying to understand how things work — and how they break.

06 :: MISSION

We try to make people aware of IT-related risks
and how dangerous they can really be.

Awareness is the cheapest defense there is. Most of what gets people owned isn't zero-days — it's a phishing email, a bad password, a reused credential, a phone call from "IT." We do workshops, demos, and casual conversations aimed at anyone who'll listen — from total beginners to seasoned sysadmins.

If you want to understand how the bad guys think (so you can stop thinking like a victim), you're in the right place.

07 :: HOW TO JOIN

There's no application. No dues. No hazing. Just two steps:

  1. Get into the IRC channel (see section 08 for the full flow — register a nick, email us, get an invite).
  2. Watch the channel for the next meetup. Venue & time get locked in a few days beforehand, right there.

When the day comes, show up. Look for the table with the laptops, lock-picks, and animated arguing. Say salam / bonjour / hey. Welcome in.

08 :: HANG OUT ONLINE

Between meetings, we keep the signal alive on IRC — the original hacker chat protocol, still going strong since 1988. Real-time, ephemeral, no algorithms, no ads, no data harvesting. The way chat was meant to be.

⚷ INVITE-ONLY CHANNEL

Our channel is locked with mode +i. You can't just walk in — a mod has to /invite you. This keeps out scanners, spambots, and the occasional script kiddie wandering through Libera looking for targets.

The onboarding is quick: register your nick, email us, wait for the invite. Usually same-day.

The Onboarding Flow

1

INSTALL AN IRC CLIENT

Pick whatever fits your setup:

  • HexChat (GUI, easiest) — hexchat.github.io
  • irssi / WeeChat (terminal, old-school) — apt install irssi or brew install weechat
  • Web client (zero install) — web.libera.chat
2

CONNECT TO LIBERA.CHAT

Point your client at the network:

Server:   irc.libera.chat
Port:     6697 (TLS — always use TLS)
Nickname: pick one, make it yours
3

REGISTER YOUR NICKNAME

This step is required. We only invite registered nicks — it's how we know you're actually you, and how we stop impersonation. Once connected:

/msg NickServ REGISTER yourpassword your@email.com

Check your email (and spam folder) for a verification code, then:

/msg NickServ VERIFY REGISTER yournick abc123xyz

You'll see "You have now verified..." — you're now a registered operative on Libera.Chat.

4

SET UP SASL (recommended)

SASL auto-identifies you the instant you connect, which means you're cloaked (IP hidden) before anyone even sees you online. It's the right way to do IRC.

Client-specific instructions are in the full IRC guide — see below.

5

REQUEST YOUR INVITE

Email us with your registered nickname and a line or two about yourself. Nothing formal — just enough that we know you're a real human with a genuine interest.

EMAIL
contact@defcon21222.org
INCLUDE
• Your registered Libera.Chat nickname
• A sentence or two about who you are
• How you heard about DC21222 (optional)
6

WAIT FOR /invite

A mod will fire an /invite in your direction, and at the same time grant you a persistent access flag so you'll never need to email us again. You'll see a server notice that looks something like:

*** [mod] invites you to #████████

The exact channel name is revealed in the invite, not on this page — a small bit of operational hygiene.

7

JOIN & INTRODUCE YOURSELF

Once you have the invite, join with:

/join #the-channel-name-from-your-invite

Say salam, bonjour, or hey — whichever suits. Read the topic, lurk for a bit, and jump in when something catches your interest.

8

FUTURE RECONNECTS :: SELF-INVITE

Once you've got the persistent flag from step 6, you're autonomous. Every time you reconnect to Libera, you invite yourself via ChanServ — no mod required, no email, no waiting.

/msg ChanServ INVITE #the-channel-name
/join #the-channel-name

Better yet, automate it. In HexChat: Network List → Libera.Chat → Edit → Connect commands, add:

msg ChanServ INVITE #the-channel-name

Now every future launch: SASL auths you, ChanServ invites you, client auto-joins. Zero friction. Welcome home.
